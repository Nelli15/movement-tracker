Movements Movements The movement forms the center of the Movement Tracker App, create one to get started. To create a movement, head to the Dashboard and either click the
add
in the left toolbar or right click and select
addNew
. The new movement will appear with the name 'Untitled Movement'. There are two ways to copy a movement.
- Copy
- Carbon Copy

Copy makes a partial copy of the movement. Anyone with access to a movement is able to copy it.
Including:
- Name
- Color
- Roles
- Modifiers
Excluding:
- Members
- Statistics
- Snapshots
- Access

Carbon Copy makes a copy of the movement in it's entirety. Only Super Editors and Owners are able to Carbon Copy a movement.
Including:
- Name
- Color
- Roles
- Modifiers
- Members
- Statistics
- Snapshots
Excluding:
- Access

To copy a movement navigate to the Dashboard and right click the movement, select either
content_copy Copy
or
content_copyCarbon Copy
. The new movement will appear as 'Copy of <Movement Name>'. There are 5 main settings that can be changed in a movement.
- Name
- Color
- Roles
- Modifiers
- Access To rename or color of the movement, right click the movement on the Dashboard. For other options see Roles, Modifiers, and Access. A movement can only be deleted by an owner of the movement, so be careful who you make an owner of your movement. Right click the movement on the Dashboard and select
deleteDelete
to delete the movement. This cannot be undone! Members Members Movements are populated by tree's of members, members can be styled, copied, moved & be a child of another member. To add a member, head to a movement and either click the
person_add
in the left toolbar or right click and select
person_addNew
. Once you fill in the members details the new member will appear under the selected parent. The '--First Member in Tree--' parent will make them a root member of the tree. There are 5 main details that can be changed in a member.
- Name
- Role
- Modifiers
- Parent
- Notes To edit a member, right click the member and select
editEdit
to change the members name, roles and modifers. To change the parent of the memeber, see Move a Member. Copy a member by right clicking it
person
and selecting
content_copyCopy
. The copied member will appear below the member that you copied.
person
 Move a member by right clicking it and selecting
Move
. The moved member will appear below the selected parent. A member can only be deleted by Super Editors and Owners. To delete a member, right click the member and select
deleteDelete
. This cannot be undone! If you are an Editor you can move the member to the --No Parent-- section instead. Roles Roles Each member has a role within a movement. Roles define the default styling applied to a member, including:
- Background Color
- Text Color
- Border Color
- Underline
- Shape

Roles are only available within the movement that they are created in. If you wish to copy all of the roles from one movement, see Copy a Movement. Only Super Editors and Owners can change roles. To add a role, head to the movement settings page
settings
right click and select
addAdd Role
. The new role will appear with the name 'Untitled Style'. There are 7 main options that can be applied to a role.
- Label
- Description
- Background Color
- Text Color
- Border Color
- Underline
- Shape
To edit a role navigate to the movement settings page
settings
, click the Role tab, click the element in the role that you wish to change and a popup will come up. Once you have changed the style, press 'Enter'. To edit a roles label or description right click and select
editEdit
. Each member is required to have one and only one role. You assign a role to a member when you create the member
person
and you can change the role assigned by right clicking the member
person
, clicking
editEdit
, and selecting the role. A role can only be deleted when it is not in use. To delete a role, navigate to the movement settings page
settings
and press the delete
delete
button in the row of the role to delete. This cannot be undone! Modifiers (Optional) Modifiers (Optional) Editors have the ability to assign modifiers to members but not change them. Assigning modifiers to members is optional. Modifiers override the styling of the role applied to a member, as such we recommend only including 1 or 2 style changes in a modifier. Styles that modifiers can override include:
- Background Color
- Text Color
- Border Color
- Underline
- Shape
- Icon
- Icon Location

Modifers are only available within the movement that they are created in. If you wish to copy all of the modifiers from one movement, see Copy a Movement. Only Super Editors and Owners can change modifiers. To add a modifier, head to the movement settings page
settings
right click and select
addAdd Modifier
. The new modifier will appear with the name 'Untitled Style'. Only Super Editors and Owners can change modifiers.There are 9 main options that can be applied to a modifier.
- Label
- Description
- Background Color
- Text Color
- Border Color
- Underline
- Shape (Rectangle or Round)
- Icon
- Icon Location To edit a modifier, navigate to the movement settings page
settings
, click the Modifier tab, make sure you enable the style override using the tickbox next to the style before editing the style, click the element in the modifier that you wish to change and a popup will come up. Once you have changed the style, press 'Enter'. Modifiers are optional. You can assign a modifier to a member when you create the member
person
and you can change the modifiers assigned by right clicking the member
person
, clicking
editEdit
, and selecting the modifier. Remove the modifier by clicking the
close
button on the modifier. A modifier can only be deleted when it is not in use. To delete a modifier, navigate to the movement settings page
settings
and press the delete button
delete
in the row of the modifier to delete. This cannot be undone! Sharing & Permissions Sharing & Permissions Only Super Editors and Owners can change sharing permissions. Sharing and Permissions allows you to share the movement with others and allow others to help keep the movement up to date. To share the movement select the
people
button in the header of a movement page. You can then invite somebody by typing their email and selecting the level of permission that their should have. (See Manage Access) There are 4 levels of permissions as detailed below:
- Viewers can only view the tree, member details, movement legend, and movement summary.
- Editors can do everything that a Viewer can do and also edit the tree, including: adding, editing, copying, and moving members.
- Super Editors can do everything that an Editor can do and also edit the roles and modifiers. They can also delete members, change sharing permissions of those who aren't Owners, and take snapshots. The main thing they can't do is delete the Movement.
- Owners can do everything! The main difference between a Super Editor and an Owner is that the Owner can delete the movement and a Super Editor cannot. We recommend never making others an Owner. To movement access, head to the movement settings page.
people
Invite Users
To invite a new user to the movement, simply enter their email into the email field, select the permission level and click send. To invite multiple users, simply seperate their emails by commas ','.
Requests
Requests appear when someone has navigated to the URL of the movement with insufficient permissions to access it, the user is then asked if they would like to request access. Once a request is in the reqests section of the access page, a Super Editor or Owner can accept and provide a suitable level of access or deny the request.
Managing Permissions
Once a user has been give permissions a Super Editor or Owner of greater or equal permission can change a users permissions. A Super Editor is able to grant at most a Super Editors level of access. To stop sharing a movement simply click the
delete
button next to the user you wish to remove.
